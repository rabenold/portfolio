Discoveries & Insights

Summary

I learned a number of things over the course of this exercise. Particularly, I found that small investors consistently account for half of purchases and sales participated in by an investor. I also learned that on average, investors classified as institutional are spending significantly more than large buyers, while small investors also spend a significant amount on average. Another thing that I learned is that medium-sized investors are spending the most on apartments with >4 units, and most investors don't seem to spend much on apartments with four units or less. Over time, the number of transactions that are conducted between two investor parties has doubled. This exercise also raised a lot of follow up questions. Particularly, I'm interested to learn more about the landscape of apartment purchases, and dig into how and why investor type might play into this. Furthermore, I'd like to spend more time looking at LLCs and Trusts, and seeing if there's anything of note in how these parties are selling or buying residential homes - and specifically the single-family and condiminium property types. I definitely struggled with this exercise, and I think that the main lesson I learned is that my lacking Tableau skills are holding me back from doing effective data wrangling and from generating interesting graphs. That's unfortunate, but at least there's opportunity for improvement over the next few assignments.
